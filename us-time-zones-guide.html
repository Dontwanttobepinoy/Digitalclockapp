US Time Zones Guide: Pacific to Eastern Time Zones

Published: December 18, 2024 Category: US Time Zones Reading Time: 7 minutes

The United States spans four main time zones: Pacific Time (PT), Mountain Time (MT), Central Time (CT), and Eastern Time (ET). Understanding these time zones is crucial for scheduling meetings, travel planning, and business operations across different regions.

Pacific Time (PT)

Pacific Time covers the westernmost states of the United States, including California, Washington, Oregon, and parts of Nevada and Idaho.

Pacific Standard Time (PST)

  • UTC Offset: UTC-8
  • Period: November to March (Standard Time)
  • Major Cities: Los Angeles, San Francisco, Seattle, Portland
  • Business Hours: 9:00 AM - 5:00 PM PST

Pacific Daylight Time (PDT)

  • UTC Offset: UTC-7
  • Period: March to November (Daylight Saving Time)
  • Note: Arizona does not observe DST

Pacific Time Conversions

Pacific to Other US Time Zones

  • PST to MST: +1 hour
  • PST to CST: +2 hours
  • PST to EST: +3 hours

Mountain Time (MT)

Mountain Time covers states in the Rocky Mountain region, including Colorado, Utah, Montana, Wyoming, and parts of other western states.

Mountain Standard Time (MST)

  • UTC Offset: UTC-7
  • Period: November to March (Standard Time)
  • Major Cities: Denver, Phoenix, Salt Lake City, Albuquerque
  • Business Hours: 9:00 AM - 5:00 PM MST

Mountain Daylight Time (MDT)

  • UTC Offset: UTC-6
  • Period: March to November (Daylight Saving Time)
  • Note: Arizona does not observe DST

Mountain Time Conversions

Mountain to Other US Time Zones

  • MST to PST: -1 hour
  • MST to CST: +1 hour
  • MST to EST: +2 hours

Central Time (CT)

Central Time covers the central region of the United States, including Texas, Illinois, Missouri, and most of the Midwest.

Central Standard Time (CST)

  • UTC Offset: UTC-6
  • Period: November to March (Standard Time)
  • Major Cities: Chicago, Houston, Dallas, Minneapolis
  • Business Hours: 9:00 AM - 5:00 PM CST

Central Daylight Time (CDT)

  • UTC Offset: UTC-5
  • Period: March to November (Daylight Saving Time)

Central Time Conversions

Central to Other US Time Zones

  • CST to PST: -2 hours
  • CST to MST: -1 hour
  • CST to EST: +1 hour

Eastern Time (ET)

Eastern Time covers the easternmost states of the United States, including New York, Florida, Georgia, and most of the East Coast.

Eastern Standard Time (EST)

  • UTC Offset: UTC-5
  • Period: November to March (Standard Time)
  • Major Cities: New York, Miami, Atlanta, Boston
  • Business Hours: 9:00 AM - 5:00 PM EST

Eastern Daylight Time (EDT)

  • UTC Offset: UTC-4
  • Period: March to November (Daylight Saving Time)

Eastern Time Conversions

Eastern to Other US Time Zones

  • EST to PST: -3 hours
  • EST to MST: -2 hours
  • EST to CST: -1 hour

Daylight Saving Time (DST)

Most of the United States observes Daylight Saving Time, which begins on the second Sunday in March and ends on the first Sunday in November.

DST Schedule

  • Spring Forward: Second Sunday in March at 2:00 AM
  • Fall Back: First Sunday in November at 2:00 AM
  • Duration: Approximately 8 months (March to November)

States Not Observing DST

  • Arizona: Does not observe DST (except Navajo Nation)
  • Hawaii: Does not observe DST
  • Territories: American Samoa, Guam, Puerto Rico, U.S. Virgin Islands

Business Applications

Meeting Scheduling Across Time Zones

When scheduling meetings across US time zones:

  • East to West: Schedule earlier in the day for East Coast participants
  • West to East: Schedule later in the day for West Coast participants
  • Optimal Times: 10:00 AM - 2:00 PM in the originating time zone
  • Account for DST: Always verify current time zone status

Travel Planning

For travel across time zones:

  • Flight Schedules: Always check local time at destination
  • Hotel Bookings: Confirm check-in times in local time
  • Business Meetings: Convert all times to local time zone
  • Jet Lag: Plan for time zone adjustment
